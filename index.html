L’Oréal Group — “Beauty & Career Confidence” Case Study

Brand Strategy • Gen Z Students • SC&IS + Marketing
Beauty Brand Strategy Student Campaign SC&IS Insight

Goal: Design a social-first campaign that connects everyday college moments (studying, interviewing, presenting) with L’Oréal products that help students feel confident and polished.

Insight: Gen Z sees beauty as part of identity and career energy, not vanity. They want routines that are fast, affordable, and “polished enough” for real life.

Strategy: “Polished Enough” Positioning

  • Show simple routines that take 5–10 minutes and use only a few staple L’Oréal products.
  • Focus on real student scenarios: 8AM midterm, club presentation, on-campus interview, part-time job.
  • Use TikTok & IG Reels to share GRWM and “confidence hacks” content with honest, low-pressure tone.

Content Pillars:

  • GRWM x Academics: “Get Ready With Me for my supply chain exam.”
  • Career Confidence: “10-minute interview look using only 3 L’Oréal products.”
  • Real Student Stories: quick clips of students sharing how small routines help them feel prepared.

Supply Chain & Information Systems Angle:

  • Use social listening (views, saves, comments like “I need this”) to flag high-demand SKUs in student markets.
  • Recommend priority SKUs (e.g., concealer, mascara, tinted serum) for campus-area retailers and drugstores.
  • Propose a simple “micro-replenishment” model: increase safety stock for top SKUs in locations with strong social engagement.

Why it’s in my portfolio: This case study shows how I combine brand storytelling with SC&IS thinking—linking content performance to demand patterns and smarter inventory decisions.
